QuotaBar — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 2, 2026

The short version

QuotaBar collects nothing. It has no network access, no accounts, no analytics, and no third-party SDKs. Everything it does happens on your Mac.

What the app reads

QuotaBar displays usage information for developer command-line tools you may have installed — Claude Code (Anthropic) and Codex CLI (OpenAI). Those tools write log files inside your home folder (~/.claude and ~/.codex). With your explicit permission — granted through the standard macOS folder picker and revocable at any time — QuotaBar reads those files to compute usage percentages, reset times, and context-window fullness.

What leaves your Mac

Nothing. QuotaBar makes no network connections. The data it reads is never transmitted, stored elsewhere, or shared with anyone, including us.

Notifications

Usage alerts are scheduled locally on your device using Apple's notification system. They are generated and delivered entirely on your Mac.

Changes

If a future version of QuotaBar ever changes any of the above, this policy will be updated before that version ships, and the App Store privacy label will reflect it.
